01 — Problem Statement

Why this project exists

A mid-sized e-commerce company was making decisions based on monthly Excel reports that were outdated, inconsistent, and took 3 days to compile. Leadership had no real-time visibility into which products, regions, or sales reps were performing — or underperforming.

The Business Problem

Sales data was scattered across 5 departments with no unified view. The company was losing revenue opportunities in high-growth regions while over-investing in saturated markets — without knowing it.

The Analytical Goal

Build a centralized, automated Sales Dashboard that gives real-time KPIs, trend analysis, and actionable business insights — reducing reporting time from 3 days to under 30 seconds.

Key Questions to Answer

Which product categories drive the most revenue? Which regions are underperforming? What is the monthly/quarterly growth trend? Who are the top 10 customers by lifetime value?

Success Metrics

Dashboard load time under 5 seconds, full-year revenue visibility, automated data refresh every 24 hours, and clear actionable recommendations for each business unit.

03 — Data Cleaning Process

Cleaning messy data

The raw data had significant quality issues. Here's the step-by-step cleaning process performed using Python (pandas) before loading into SQL and Power BI.

01

Remove Duplicate Records

Found 1,247 duplicate order_ids caused by CRM sync errors. Used pandas drop_duplicates(subset=['order_id']) to remove them, keeping the latest timestamp record.

02

Handle Missing Values

342 rows had NULL in the 'region' column. Filled using a customer_id join to the customers table. 89 rows with NULL amounts were dropped as they couldn't be imputed reliably.

03

Standardize Date Formats

Dates were stored in 3 different formats (DD/MM/YYYY, MM-DD-YYYY, YYYY-MM-DD). Used pd.to_datetime(df['date'], dayfirst=True) and converted all to ISO 8601 standard.

04

Fix Categorical Inconsistencies

"Electronics", "electronics", "ELECTRONICS", "Electrnics" — 14 such spelling variants found across category column. Used a mapping dictionary and .str.strip().str.title() to standardize.

05

Outlier Detection & Treatment

Used IQR method to detect outliers in order amounts. 23 records with amounts > ₹10 lakh were verified with the business team — 18 were genuine bulk orders (retained), 5 were data entry errors (corrected).

06

Feature Engineering

Created new columns: profit_margin (amount − cost_price), order_month, order_quarter, customer_tenure_days, and is_repeat_customer flag for segmentation analysis.

04 — SQL Queries

Core SQL Queries

All analysis was performed in PostgreSQL. Below are the key queries used to power each dashboard panel.

Q1 Monthly Revenue Trend
-- Monthly revenue aggregation with YoY growth
SELECT
    DATE_TRUNC('month', order_date) AS month,
    SUM(amount)                        AS total_revenue,
    COUNT(order_id)                    AS total_orders,
    ROUND(AVG(amount), 2)             AS avg_order_value,
    SUM(amount) - LAG(SUM(amount))
        OVER (ORDER BY DATE_TRUNC('month', order_date))
                                       AS mom_growth
FROM orders
WHERE status = 'Completed'
  AND order_date BETWEEN '2024-01-01' AND '2024-12-31'
GROUP BY 1
ORDER BY month;
Q2 Top 10 Customers by LTV
-- Customer lifetime value with segmentation
SELECT
    c.customer_id,
    c.customer_name,
    c.region,
    COUNT(o.order_id)       AS total_orders,
    SUM(o.amount)           AS lifetime_value,
    MAX(o.order_date)       AS last_order_date,
    CASE
        WHEN SUM(o.amount) > 500000 THEN 'Platinum'
        WHEN SUM(o.amount) > 200000 THEN 'Gold'
        ELSE 'Silver'
    END                     AS customer_tier
FROM customers c
JOIN orders o ON c.customer_id = o.customer_id
WHERE o.status = 'Completed'
GROUP BY c.customer_id, c.customer_name, c.region
ORDER BY lifetime_value DESC
LIMIT 10;
Q3 Region-wise Performance
-- Regional performance vs target with variance
SELECT
    c.region,
    SUM(o.amount)                          AS actual_revenue,
    SUM(sr.target)                         AS target_revenue,
    ROUND(
        (SUM(o.amount) - SUM(sr.target))
        * 100.0 / NULLIF(SUM(sr.target), 0)
    , 2)                                    AS achievement_pct,
    RANK() OVER (ORDER BY SUM(o.amount) DESC) AS rank
FROM orders o
JOIN customers c   ON o.customer_id = c.customer_id
JOIN sales_reps sr ON c.region = sr.region
WHERE o.status = 'Completed'
GROUP BY c.region
ORDER BY actual_revenue DESC;
05 — Analysis

What the data revealed

After cleaning and querying the data, several clear patterns emerged that directly translate into business action.

+34%

Electronics Growth

Electronics revenue grew 34% YoY, driven primarily by smartphone and audio accessories. Q4 alone contributed 42% of the annual category revenue.

North

Top Performing Region

The North region achieved 118% of its annual target — the only region to exceed targets. South region was the biggest miss at 71% achievement.

₹2.4L

Avg Platinum LTV

The top 5% of customers (Platinum tier) account for 38% of total revenue, with an average lifetime value of ₹2.4 lakh per customer.

Dec

Peak Revenue Month

December was the highest-grossing month at ₹3.2M (13% of annual revenue), with a 2.8× spike in orders during the festive sale window (Dec 20–27).

6.4%

Churn Rate Reduced

Customer churn dropped from 8.5% to 6.4% year-over-year — attributed to the loyalty program introduced in Q2 2024, which enrolled 4,200+ customers.

22 min

Avg Order Processing

Average order-to-dispatch time improved from 48 hours to 22 minutes after the warehouse automation upgrade, reducing cancellations by 31%.

07 — Business Insights

Actionable recommendations

Based on the analysis, here are 6 concrete recommendations presented to the leadership team — with estimated revenue impact.

📈

Double down on Electronics in Q4

Electronics drove 42% of Q4 revenue. Recommend increasing inventory and marketing budget by 30% in Oct–Dec window, targeting the ₹15K–₹50K price band which showed highest volume growth.

🗺️

Investigate South Region underperformance

South achieved only 71% of target despite comparable market size to North. Root cause: only 3 active sales reps vs 9 in North. Recommend hiring 4 additional reps in Karnataka and Tamil Nadu.

👑

Invest in Platinum customer retention

Top 5% of customers drive 38% of revenue. A dedicated account manager program and exclusive early access to new products could improve their retention rate from 82% to 95%+, adding ₹3.2M annually.

🔄

Reactivation campaign for dormant customers

2,841 customers have not ordered in 180+ days. A targeted win-back email campaign with a 15% discount coupon, based on their last product category, is projected to reactivate 18–22% of them.

🏷️

Optimize pricing for Home Goods category

Home Goods has the highest margin (34%) but only 20% revenue share. Price sensitivity analysis shows a 10% price reduction could increase volume by 45%, improving overall category contribution.

⚡

Automate daily reporting pipeline

Current manual reporting takes 3 person-days/month. Implementing a fully automated ETL pipeline (Python + PostgreSQL + Power BI scheduled refresh) saves 36 person-days annually and eliminates human error.
